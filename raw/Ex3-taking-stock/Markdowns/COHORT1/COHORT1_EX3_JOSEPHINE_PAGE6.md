# Ex3b - Open Q&A [Asynchronous]

**Instructions**
Looking back at your history with LLMs, answer two open-ended questions based on the session history:

**Deadline**
This exercise is due for October 7th, you're asked to bring it completed.

**What has the machine done for you?**
Describe how the LLM has been helpful and useful to your work so far

It helped me a lot to speed up my work by me delegating tasks such as translating and writing of emails so that I can focus on other aspects of my work that are more important.
I mostly use it for reformulating sentences for my thesis or other papers, writing emails and using the right level of formality, as well as for translating.
Usually, these tasks would take up quite a bit of time but due to the LLM's it is a lot faster.
Sometimes I use LLM's also to brainstorm ideas, when I am stuck. In this case LLM's were useful to give me some inspiration for topics or issues that I could adress in articles or arguments I could put forward in my thesis. It works a bit like having a conversation partner with whom you can check, whether your ideas make sense.

**What have you done for the machine?**
Reflect on the work and assistance you've provided to get good results out of the LLM.

One thing I found to be very important is to give some instructions on for example style or type of answer I want. If I do not give such details, the results will often not exactly fit my expectations.
However if I include words such as "Thoroughly re-phrase" and "use a formal but friendly tone", the results were a lot better.
Also one thing I usually do, especially when I ask for information is to ask for the sources as well.
ChatGPT has a tendency to not refer to sources or even making up information.
Therefore, I found it crucial to explicitly ask for sources to be able to crosscheck the results. Once it provides me sources, or links I always go into the articles & web-pages to ensure that the information is indeed true.
