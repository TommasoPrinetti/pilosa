# Ex5a - A thought experiment

### Instructions

When we ask the machine something, the machine answers. What is left when we subtract the fact that a machine answers from the fact that we get an answer?

Practically speaking, we often say: "I asked *ChatGPT* to write an email", or even: "I asked it to write an email". In the following exercise, we will ask:

**what would happen if we described our relationship to the LLM without using words like "machine", "AI", "LLM", "ChatGPT" or "it"?**

We will review our memorable conversations (from Ex03), read them, and wonder: "Who is asking? Who is answering?". For each conversation, we will identify an excerpt that exemplifies who is writing (e.g., an "assistant" would answer "This is a draft for your email"). We will identify one to three excerpts for answers and questions.

When trying to describe who is writing or speaking, we are free to choose any word except the subtracted words ("machine", "AI", "LLM", "ChatGPT"). This could be a professional title ("an assistant"), an online service ("Wikipedia"), a corporation ("McKinsey"), or even an undefined entity ("a ghost").
