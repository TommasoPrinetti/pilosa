# Ex5c - Confronting perspectives

## Instructions

You have now built your lexicon of terms through a series of subtractions. What do you think of the final terms?
Do they adequately describe who is writing/speaking?
Do some feel uncanny or weird to use? Why?

The final terms accurately reflect the topic I inquired about.
However I do not think that they convey the identity or tone of
the speaker.

(1) E.g. Throughout all conversations the tone of "the one asking/
speaking" is quite similar, regardless of the subject matter.
For example, I would phrase my questions differently if I
knew an actual person or expert were responding, rather
than "the machine". I'd be way more polite and tailored.

(2) I feel like the terms almost became too specific to
capture topic and tone for some conversations who is writing/
speaking. E.g. the nature of the conversation was generic,
well-captured by "stressed student", and did not
require much further specification for the sake of
specification.

⊗ E.g. it seems clear that it is "me" speaking and not someone I
mimick, independent of the topic.

Think back to your fellow co-inquirers' responses. What stood out to you? Did they use terms that you would never use? Did you have some in common?

(1) I tend to see ChatGPT as "the expert", e.g. someone
with more knowledge than myself. For some others it seemed
to be reverse; Broadly ChatGPT as the assistant and they
as the expert

(2) Especially in the first round of term identification
we had many terms in common, likely stemming from
the fact that we are all students and thus face similar
problems.

(3) Some people used terms such as history book or
wikipedia. I would not use these terms as they feel more
tangible than I perceive ChatGPT to be; E.g. with books I
