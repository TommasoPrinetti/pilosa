Instructions
Looking back at your history with LLMs, answer two open-ended questions based on the session history:

Deadline

**What has the machine done for you?**
Describe how the LLM has been helpful and useful to your work so far

First, I notice that I use ChatGPT exclusively for study- and/or professional tasks. In this context, there are two main tasks that stand out: (1) Writing assistance - as in paraphrasing, drafting and providing specific feedback, (2) and coding assistance ranging from troubleshooting to directly providing lines of code. Interestingly, the nature of the writing assistance diverges from the one for coding. For writing, I hardly adopt ChatGPT's suggestions and rather use it as a mechanism to verify the "quality" of my work. Only when I am very time constrained I adopt ChatGPT's suggestions. For coding, on the other hand I often just provide the problem and copy-paste the code provided by ChatGPT. Thus, ChatGPT here has allowed me to code "above my level".

**What have you done for the machine?**
Reflect on the work and assistance you've provided to get good results out of the LLM.

I have not actively thought about how to optimize prompts for ChatGPT. Rather I would describe the process of achieving good results as quite iterative: I have a certain output in mind and adapt the prompt until I receive it. Over time, however, this has led me to try to specify my problem and question as much as possible, and at the same time to provide a structure to ChatGPT's answer: e.g. I ask it to elaborate / stick to xyz aspects. Further October 2023, I had to do some googling to find out that ChatGPT's data is limited to 2021 data, which explained insufficient outputs regarding recent events.
