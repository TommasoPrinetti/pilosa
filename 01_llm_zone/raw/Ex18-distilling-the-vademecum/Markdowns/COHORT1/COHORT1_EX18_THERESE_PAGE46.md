# Ex18 - ChatGPT as a therapist

February 2023 - Break
ChatGPT as a therapist

I met a guy who explained to me he was using ChatGPT as his therapist.
He spoke with it hundred of hours. He is a Success manager, so he
hasn't the technical academic knowledge, but he is kind of geek, knows
how to code and to prompt. He knows how ChatGPT works with statistics.
He is supposed to have a critical point of view on the machine.

At the same time, he anthropomorphises a lot ChatGPT and has the feeling
it "understands" him well, almost in the human sense of understanding.

-> trust in the machine
-> mix of emotions and rational
-> on the tightrope between "it's statistical" and "it understands me"

=> more important: what you think the machine does, rather than what
it can really do.

It was creepy and I felt ill-at-ease. I found it dangerous to give
such a central role in your well-being and way to thought to a machine
that doesn't understand the context, answers statistically and has biases.
