# Ex18 - Distilling the Vademecum [Asynchronous]

Instructions
As we approach the final phase of our experiments—the Debrief block—
we invite you to craft a reflective narrative that explores the role of LLMs in
your daily practices, personal reflections, and professional contexts.

This narrative is not a mere recounting of the experiments; instead, it explores
how you engage with LLMs and how they shape your experiences.

This first exercise in the Debrief block aims to produce a preliminary draft of your
personal narrative by synthesizing material from your vademecum and your broader
history of interactions with LLMs. At the end of this exercise, you should have a short
booklet containing a curated collection of pivotal vademecum pages, annotated notes,
sketches, screenshots, and digital artifacts that document the turning points and
everyday encounters that have defined your evolving relationship with these tools.

(1) The first step is to review and select materials from the vademecum.
Identify and extract pages that capture pivotal moments or common
experiences that have marked your journey with LLMs.

These pages should serve as tangible examples—representing
turning points or everyday encounters—that have contributed
to your evolving relationship with these tools.

(2) The second step is to expand your archive. Gather the additional blank pages we
provided. Use these pages to add your notes, sketches, screenshots, or digital
artifacts (such as links to key ChatGPT conversations). For example, include
a brief note on a meaningful conversation with a colleague or a screenshot
that encapsulates an essential moment in your interaction with an LLM.

(3) The last and final step is to construct your booklet. Organize the selected vademecum
pages alongside your supplementary materials into a cohesive mini-booklet. The
booklet should visually and textually represent the evolution of your engagement with
LLMs. Let this collection serve as the material evidence that underpins your narrative.

In our next session, you will present the first draft of your
story by going through this small booklet.
