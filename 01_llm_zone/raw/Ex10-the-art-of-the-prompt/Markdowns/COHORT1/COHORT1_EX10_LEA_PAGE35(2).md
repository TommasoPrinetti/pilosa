# Ex10 - The Art of the Prompt [Asynchronous]

### Instructions

In this exercise, you will learn about a specific prompting technique, put it into practice, and write a short report about your experience.

Each of you will choose a prompting technique at random. Inside a shared directory, you will find a paper explaining the prompting technique, as well as a template for slides. For our next session, you will:

1.  **Read the paper explaining the prompting technique.** We also recommend that you read section 1.1 of Schulhoff et al, 2024 (pp. 5-7), called "What is a prompt?", but it is optional.
2.  **Use the prompting technique throughout the week**, to get a better feel for it
3.  **Answer some questions about the technique**, by editing the slides directly

During our next session, **you will present your overall experience of the prompting technique, using the slides (short 5-minute presentation).** You can add more details about your experience in your oral presentation, but the slides should be concise and to the point. Your slides will be printed on a single A4 sheet and distributed to everyone at the beginning of the session.

The slides need to be completed **by November 15th.**
