# Ex5b - What Remains After Subtraction

*Reflect on the final terms*

Interestingly enough, the process of subtraction led us toward tailoring our wordings better while reckoning with the similar roles we gave to LLMs and ourselves in our, contrastingly, very dissimilar interactions with the model. Due to our different works, our requests are different in nature, yet we all related to a use of LLMs that was close to a "learning" curve approach where LLMs either play the role of someone explaining or testing us. This represents the majority of our interactions. Through subtraction, the final terms highlighted revealed both a specialisation of the model's roles as well as a personification of its expected position.

The selection of the final terms reveal how we seemingly treat LLMs as AGI while it is not; we distance ourself of the strengths and weaknesses LLMs have in our interactions with them since they seem "personified-machines" with whom interactions are anthropomorphised.

For this reason, reflecting on the term "subtraction" I realise that the event rather applied to us: we temporarily subtracted our awareness of what is at stake in the human-machine interaction to replace the model with an all-encompassing "label", like teacher or expert, to reciprocate social relationships we are familiar with while maintaining this "tool as a service"* mindset.

* echoes to "crime as a service" concept
