# Ex9b - The Verdict Is In

**Explain Your Judgement**
*Write here*

Generally Sloth is interesting for me. It really brief, concise and on average gives something you can always work with. Really perfect for brainstorming quick ideas, concise communication like e-mails and related tasks. I recommend for short routine tasks.

Second comes Dogon. I think its a wordy LLM that can easily lose a user if not well prompted. Seems to work really well for law. I loved the detail and sample contractual clauses it gave me for task 3, though it proved that it can be long (the outcome). Not reliable for quick tasks like e-mailing or tasks that need you to brainstorm or communicate concisely. I would probably use it for research when I have time not for urgent tasks. Also, not for practicals like draft or prep a memo.... I think it can just bore you.

Giraffe: For summarizing content, giraffe was a bit all over the place. Brief but I could not follow. Performed even worse with the email (lacked a human touch or professional sense, no subject line or a proper salutation (dear x/y-)). So its poor at correspondence/communication. Surprisingly it improved in making a memo (or a legal advisory). Included an executive summary so I knew the contents of what I was reading and the outline resembled what we do usually in law firms. Giraffe shocked me on this. It also did well with contracts. It was able to understand the transaction, then gave me the contracts applicable to each stakeholder before delving into the clauses. Language was technical though.

Turkey: didn't work with content summary (based on uploaded content). The clauses it gave me were general to project finance. Didn't take into account the detailed outline of the transaction that I provided. However it won with the memo -> was well organized. Each option had everything beneath it, easy to follow too. For correspondence I would not use it. It was off with the tone and didn't market me as a great candidate to be considered. Would use it again for structuring my memos.
