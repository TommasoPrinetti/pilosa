# Ex9a - Judgment Day

**Exercise 09x - Weighing the Evidence**

In this exercise, you will judge the results of your chosen four tasks (Ex07).

For each task, you will get a playground and four cards, representing four different results: three from LLMs, and one from a human. To ensure a fair judgment, the results will be blinded, and each performer will be represented by an animal (a giraffe □, a dragon □, a sloth □, and a turkey □).

You will act as a judge and rank the four results of a given task. To judge a performance, you can scan the QR code at the back of each card, which will take you to a Google Document with the results. You can also find the task folder inside a shared directory. You will place the four cards on the playground, organizing them from left to right, left being the worst performance and right the best. You can use the spacing between cards to signify the difference between performances: two cards can be on top of each other if the quality of the results is equivalent; one card can be very far from the other three if its result is far better (or far worse).

**Exercise 09g - The Verdict is In**

You will now share and explain your ranking to the whole group. The co-inquirer who performed the task will be called as an expert witness to share their experience. The rest of the group can chime in to discuss the ranking and question the judge about their criteria. The judge can alter their ranking after this collective exchange if they'd like.
