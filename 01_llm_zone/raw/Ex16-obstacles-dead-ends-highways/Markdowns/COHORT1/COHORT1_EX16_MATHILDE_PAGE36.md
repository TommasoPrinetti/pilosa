# Ex16 - Obstacles, Dead Ends, Highways

## Instructions
This exercise invites you to reflect on how you navigate LLMs when you pursue a specific goal. You will do this by mapping out three moments you have encountered during the Imitation Game: Obstacles, Deadends, and Highways.

## Dead Ends
Dead Ends are moments when you feel stuck, unable to progress, or at a loss to know what to do next.

To begin, consider the earliest signs that convinced you there was no way around the problem.

> Did the LLM keep returning the same answers despite multiple attempts, or did it contradict itself in a way that made further progress impossible?

Next, focus on the specific approaches you tried before concluding it was truly a dead end.

> Did you insist to make the LLM do what you wanted to do? Did you explore other strategies?

Reflect on the point at which you recognized this was beyond a typical obstacle:

> What clues or patterns told you it was time to stop?

Finally, consider the emotional and intellectual toll of encountering a dead end.

> Were you frustrated, resigned, exhausted?
